Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to my recycling after collection? +

Your recyclables are taken to a materials recovery facility where they are sorted, cleaned, and processed into raw materials for manufacturing new products.

Can I recycle plastic bags? +

Plastic bags are not accepted in kerbside recycling bins as they jam sorting machines. Please return them to supermarket collection points instead.

How do I dispose of old electronics safely? +

E-waste should never go in household bins. Contact us to schedule a hazardous waste pickup, or visit one of our certified drop-off centres in your area.

What is composting and how do I start? +

Composting turns organic waste (food scraps, garden clippings) into rich soil amendment. Start with a small bin, add layers of green and brown waste, and turn weekly. We offer free starter kits!

Do I need to rinse containers before recycling? +

Yes — food contamination can spoil entire loads of recyclables. A quick rinse is all that's needed. Dry containers before placing them in the recycling bin.

What are common waste management problems for businesses +

Common issues invlude lack of employee training, ineffiecient waste segregation leading to high costs, and failure to comply with regulations.

What should I do if my bin wasn't collected? +

If your bin was missed, please contact us within 24 hours. We will arrange a re-collection at no extra charge and investigate the issue with our team.

Is there a fee for hazardous waste disposal? +

Small quantities of household hazardous waste (batteries, bulbs) can be dropped off free of charge. Large quantities or commercial hazardous waste incurs a handling fee.
